A journey of becoming

My story began before the code.

It began with a little girl surrounded by love, curiosity, music, books, and dreams. Scroll through the moments that shaped her into a software engineer, educator, researcher, and lifelong learner.

↓ Follow the journey

The beginning

A little girl arrived, already deeply loved.

I was born as the eldest and only daughter in a family of two siblings. With my younger brother beside me and parents who loved and encouraged me endlessly, my earliest world was filled with warmth, safety, and the freedom to dream.

01

School years

Learning became her first superpower.

I was a dedicated student who genuinely enjoyed learning. Scholarships throughout my school years became more than awards—they became reminders that consistency, discipline, and curiosity could carry me toward bigger possibilities.

02

Childhood energy

She ran, played, and learned to compete.

Badminton gave me movement, focus, and joy. It was one of the places where childhood energy turned into confidence, coordination, and the determination to keep trying.

Badminton Energy Focus
03

Creative childhood

She danced, sang, and discovered expression.

Before technology became my language, creativity was. I danced and sang as a child, learning how confidence, rhythm, imagination, and emotion could be expressed without needing to explain everything in words.

Dancing Singing Creativity
04

Stories and imagination

Books and stories made her world larger.

The works of Rabindranath Tagore and Humayun Ahmed shaped my imagination. Later, stories such as Harry Potter, Game of Thrones, and Friends became familiar worlds I loved returning to.

Tagore Humayun Ahmed Harry Potter Game of Thrones Friends
05

The turning point

Then she chose Computer Science and Engineering.

That decision changed the direction of my life. I discovered that code was not merely syntax—it was a way to build, solve, improve, and turn ideas into systems people could use.

06

Professional beginning

The student became a Software Engineer.

I entered the professional world building software, strengthening my technical skills, and learning how thoughtful systems can solve practical problems and improve real experiences.

07

Sharing knowledge

The engineer stepped into a classroom.

As a University Faculty Member, I taught, mentored students, developed learning experiences, and discovered how meaningful it could be to help someone else understand, grow, and believe in their own ability.

08

A new country

She crossed an ocean to begin again.

I moved to the United States to pursue a Master of Science in Computer Science at the University of South Dakota. It was a chapter of courage, adaptation, advanced learning, research, and becoming more capable than I had previously imagined.

09

Today

Now she builds technology at ITS, USD.

Today, I work as a Software Engineer with Information Technology Services at the University of South Dakota. I build enterprise solutions, accessible web experiences, integrations, reporting systems, and user-focused applications while continuing to explore AI and machine learning through research.

10

Every line of code still carries a piece of the little girl who simply loved learning.

She grew, traveled, taught, researched, and built—but the curiosity that started the journey never left. This is not the end of the story. It is another beginning.
